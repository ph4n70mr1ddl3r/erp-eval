## W83. Marketing Campaign Planning, Execution & Performance Measurement

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Campaign calendar milestone; or ad-hoc campaign request from C-suite / Category Manager |
| **Frequency** | 6 major bi-monthly campaigns/year + 12 monthly hot-deal cycles + ad-hoc campaigns (store openings, seasonal pushes, competitive response) |
| **Volume** | ~18–24 planned campaigns/year + ~5–10 ad-hoc campaigns; each campaign involves 2–8 channels and 200–500+ SKUs |
| **Owner** | Marketing Campaign Manager |
| **Participants** | Campaign Manager, Content Manager, CMO, VP Merchandising, Category Managers, Pricing Analysts, Store Ops Director, Ecom Team, external creative / media agencies |

### Background

BuildRight's ~24-person Marketing team operates across brand, promotions, loyalty, ecommerce, digital, content, and customer service. While W13 covers promotional pricing execution and W50 covers product content management, there is no end-to-end operational workflow for marketing campaign management — from strategic brief through creative production, channel execution, budget tracking, and post-campaign ROI measurement. This workflow bridges the gap between merchandising strategy (W1, W13, W32) and customer-facing execution across all channels.

### Campaign Types

| Campaign Type | Frequency | Channels | Typical Budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Major bi-monthly sale event** | 6/year | In-store signage, POS materials, email, SMS, social media, website banners, digital ads | PHP 2–5M/event |
| **Monthly hot deals** | 12/year | Email, SMS, website, in-store shelf talkers | PHP 200–500K/cycle |
| **New store opening** | ~10–15/year | Local radio, flyers, social media geo-targeted ads, SMS blast to nearby loyalty members, PR | PHP 500K–1M/opening |
| **Seasonal push** | 4/year (aligned with W32) | Full multi-channel: TV, digital, print, in-store events, email, SMS, social | PHP 3–8M/season |
| **Loyalty member engagement** | Monthly | Email, app push notification, in-store POS prompts | PHP 100–300K/month |
| **Competitive response** | Ad-hoc | Social media, email, SMS, local radio | PHP 200–500K |
| **Community / CSR event** | 2–4/year | PR, social media, in-store | PHP 100–300K |

### Phase 1: Campaign Planning & Brief (T-8 to T-6 weeks before campaign launch)

| # | Activity | Role (R) | Role (A) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Category Manager or VP Merchandising submits campaign brief request to Campaign Manager: campaign objective (traffic, revenue, margin, clearance, brand awareness), target customer segment, proposed SKU list, desired timing, and rough budget expectation | Category Manager | VP Merchandising | 1 hour/brief |
| 2 | Campaign Manager reviews brief with CMO; assesses feasibility against campaign calendar (avoid overlapping campaigns), budget availability, and resource capacity (creative team workload, external agency availability) | Campaign Manager | CMO | 30 min/brief |
| 3 | Campaign Manager develops campaign brief document: (a) campaign name and code (for system tracking and cost allocation), (b) objectives with measurable KPIs (target revenue lift, foot traffic increase, ecommerce conversion rate, new loyalty sign-ups), (c) target audience (all customers, loyalty tiers, trade accounts, geographic segments), (d) channel plan — which channels (in-store, email, SMS, social media, digital ads, print, radio, PR), (e) promotional mechanics (percentage off, bundle deals, gift-with-purchase, loyalty points multiplier, clearance pricing), (f) SKU list and pricing per W13, (g) timeline — key dates for creative review, material production, distribution, and go-live, (h) budget allocation by channel with estimated cost per channel, (i) success metrics and measurement plan | Campaign Manager | CMO | 4–8 hours/campaign |
| 4 | CMO reviews and approves campaign brief; VP Merchandising confirms SKU list and pricing strategy; CFO approves budget for campaigns exceeding PHP 1M | CMO / VP Merchandising / CFO | CEO (if budget > PHP 5M) | 1 hour/campaign |
| 5 | Campaign Manager creates campaign record in system: campaign code, objectives, KPIs, budget, timeline, assigned team members, linked promo pricing records (W13), and linked SKU list; system generates campaign cost center for expense tracking | Campaign Manager | CMO | 30 min/campaign |

### Phase 2: Creative Production & Asset Development (T-6 to T-2 weeks)

| # | Activity | Role (R) | Role (A) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Campaign Manager assigns creative deliverables: (a) in-store POS materials (posters, shelf talkers, banners, endcap displays, outdoor signage), (b) digital assets (website banners, social media posts/stories, email templates, digital ad creatives), (c) print materials (flyers, newspaper inserts, direct mailers), (d) SMS/email copy, (e) press releases or PR materials (if applicable) | Campaign Manager | CMO | 1 hour/campaign |
| 7 | Content Manager and Content Specialists produce or coordinate creative assets: (a) in-house for standard materials (email templates, social media posts, SMS copy, shelf talkers using templates), (b) external creative agency for major campaigns (TV/radio spots, high-end print, complex digital campaigns) per W62 service contract; agency briefed with brand guidelines, campaign messaging framework, and deliverable specifications | Content Manager / External Agency | CMO | 1–3 weeks/campaign |
| 7a | Content Manager ensures all campaign product content (images, descriptions, specifications) is complete for campaign SKUs in PIM per W50; prioritizes campaign SKUs in the content queue; escalates incomplete content to Category Manager with T-2 week deadline | Content Manager | CMO | Part of W50 |
| 8 | Campaign Manager reviews all creative assets against brand guidelines: consistent visual identity, correct pricing (match W13/W40 system prices), correct SKU descriptions, legal disclaimers (BIR-compliant pricing disclosures, promo terms and conditions), and inclusive language | Campaign Manager | CMO | 2–4 hours/campaign |
| 9 | CMO approves final creative assets; Campaign Manager archives approved assets in system linked to campaign record | CMO | CEO (for major brand campaigns) | 30 min/campaign |

### Phase 3: Channel Execution & Distribution (T-2 to T-0 weeks)

| # | Activity | Role (R) | Role (A) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | **In-store materials**: Campaign Manager coordinates printing and distribution of POS materials to 200 stores — centralized printing at HQ, shipped to stores via DC delivery trucks (W4) or direct courier for urgent materials; includes installation instructions and display planogram for promotional displays; Store Operations Director confirms receipt with Store Managers | Campaign Manager / Store Ops Director | COO | 1 week distribution |
| 11 | **Digital channels**: (a) Ecom Team uploads website banners and landing pages to ecommerce platform; configures campaign-specific landing page with promo SKUs and pricing per W13, (b) Campaign Manager schedules social media posts via social media management tool (Meta Business Suite, etc.) with approved creatives and copy, (c) Campaign Manager configures email campaign in email marketing platform: segment audience per campaign brief, schedule send dates, set up A/B test variants if applicable, (d) Campaign Manager configures SMS blast via SMS gateway provider for loyalty member segments | Campaign Manager / Ecom Team | CMO | 2–4 hours/channel |
| 12 | **Traditional media**: Campaign Manager coordinates with media buying agency (or direct media placement) for radio spots, print ads, and digital ads; confirms placement schedule, flight dates, and audience targeting parameters; media costs tracked to campaign cost center | Campaign Manager / Media Agency | CMO | Per W62 contract |
| 13 | **Loyalty integration**: Campaign Manager coordinates with Loyalty Manager to configure campaign-specific loyalty mechanics per W17 — e.g., double-points weekends, bonus points for specific categories, tier-exclusive early access, member-only pricing; Loyalty Manager configures in loyalty engine and tests before go-live | Campaign Manager / Loyalty Manager | CMO | 2 hours/campaign |
| 14 | **System readiness check** (T-3 days): Campaign Manager verifies with IT that (a) promotional pricing is loaded in POS per W13.5, (b) ecommerce campaign pages are live and pricing matches, (c) email and SMS campaigns are scheduled and tested, (d) campaign tracking tags/pixels are correctly implemented for digital performance measurement | Campaign Manager / IT | CIO | 2 hours/campaign |

| 22 | System archives campaign record with full documentation: brief, creative assets, performance data, cost tracking, and post-campaign report; available for future campaign planning reference | System | — | Automated |

## W104. Loyalty Program Financial Governance & Periodic Review

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Quarterly loyalty program review calendar; or ad-hoc triggered by significant points liability change, competitive loyalty program launch, or program design change request |
| **Frequency** | Quarterly financial review; annual program design review |
| **Volume** | 600,000 members; ~PHP 1.2–1.8B cumulative points liability; ~50M points earned/month |
| **Owner** | Loyalty Manager (operations); Cost Accountant (financial); CMO (strategic) |
| **Participants** | Loyalty Manager, Cost Accountant, Controller, CMO, CFO, Marketing Analytics |

### Background

W17 (Customer Loyalty Program Operations) covers day-to-day loyalty operations — enrollment, earning, redemption, tier management, and fraud detection. However, there is no periodic governance workflow reviewing the financial health of the loyalty program. With a cumulative points liability of PHP 1.2–1.8B on the balance sheet (deferred revenue), 600,000 members, and a program cost of ~1% of revenue, the loyalty program is a material financial obligation requiring regular oversight. This workflow covers financial reporting, liability management, breakage analysis, tier economics, program ROI, and strategic design decisions.

### Steps

### Quarterly Financial Review

| # | Activity | Role (R) | Role (A) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Liability reporting**: Cost Accountant prepares quarterly loyalty liability report: (a) total outstanding points balance and estimated liability (outstanding points × PHP 1.00 redemption value × expected redemption rate ~75–85%), (b) quarterly points earned vs. redeemed vs. expired (waterfall), (c) deferred revenue balance vs. prior quarter — trend analysis, (d) liability as % of trailing 12-month revenue (benchmark: should remain stable, not growing as % of revenue), (e) breakage rate (points expired ÷ points earned) — compare to PFRS 15 assumption used in W17.4 allocation, (f) deferred revenue adequacy — is the recorded deferred revenue sufficient to cover expected redemptions? | Cost Accountant | Controller | 2 hours/quarter |
| 2 | **Program engagement metrics**: Loyalty Manager prepares quarterly engagement report: (a) active member rate (members with ≥ 1 transaction in trailing 90 days), (b) enrollment trend (new sign-ups per month, channel breakdown: in-store vs. online), (c) redemption rate (points redeemed ÷ points available for redemption), (d) tier distribution (Bronze/Silver/Gold/Platinum member count and revenue contribution per tier), (e) average points balance per member by tier, (f) earn-to-burn ratio (points earned ÷ points redeemed — ratio > 1.0 means liability is growing), (g) member spend lift — do loyalty members spend more per transaction than non-members? (target: ≥ 15% higher ATV) | Loyalty Manager | CMO | 2 hours/quarter |
| 3 | **Program cost analysis**: Cost Accountant calculates quarterly program cost: (a) points redemption cost (value of discounts given at POS), (b) deferred revenue allocation impact on gross revenue recognition, (c) program administration cost ( Loyalty Manager time, system licensing, card printing, communication), (d) promotional loyalty costs (double-points weekends, bonus points per W17.10), (e) total program cost as % of qualifying revenue; benchmark against industry norms (1.0–1.5% for Philippine retail) | Cost Accountant | Controller | 1 hour/quarter |
| 4 | **Tier economics review**: Loyalty Manager and Cost Accountant analyze tier-level economics: (a) revenue per member by tier, (b) margin per member by tier (after loyalty discount, promotional costs, and tier-specific benefits), (c) upgrade/downgrade rates between tiers, (d) tier retention rate (% of members maintaining or upgrading tier year-over-year), (e) Platinum/Gold tier cost-benefit analysis — do high-tier benefits (exclusive pricing, priority service) drive sufficient incremental margin to justify the cost? | Loyalty Manager / Cost Accountant | CMO / Controller | 1 hour/quarter |
| 5 | **CMO and CFO joint review**: CMO (program strategy) and CFO (financial stewardship) review quarterly loyalty program performance: (a) is the liability growing sustainably?, (b) is engagement improving or declining?, (c) is the program cost within budget?, (d) are tier economics viable?, (e) competitive loyalty benchmarking — how does BuildRight's program compare to competitors (earn rate, redemption value, tier benefits)?; agree on action items (e.g., adjust earn rate, change tier thresholds, introduce new redemption options, run re-engagement campaign for dormant members) | CMO / CFO | CEO | 1 hour/quarter |

### Annual Program Design Review

| # | Activity | Role (R) | Role (A) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Loyalty Manager prepares annual program review document: (a) full-year financial summary (liability, cost, breakage, ROI), (b) program engagement trend over 3 years, (c) member segmentation analysis — heavy redeemers, points hoarders, dormant members, new members, (d) competitive program analysis — what are competitors offering?, (e) member satisfaction data from W65 CSAT surveys specific to loyalty, (f) proposed program changes for next year (earn rate, tier thresholds, new benefits, redemption catalog expansion, partnership opportunities) | Loyalty Manager | CMO | 8 hours/year |
| 7 | CMO, CFO, and CEO review annual program design; approve or modify proposed changes; changes to earn rate or redemption value require Board awareness due to balance sheet impact | CMO / CFO | CEO / Board | 2 hours/year |
| 8 | If program changes approved: Loyalty Manager coordinates implementation timeline with IT and Marketing; system configuration changes deployed; member communication plan executed (30-day advance notice for changes affecting earn rate or tier thresholds); updated program terms published on website and in-store | Loyalty Manager / IT | CMO | Per change scope |

### System Touchpoints
- Quarterly loyalty liability report: outstanding points, deferred revenue, breakage waterfall, liability trend (W104.1)
- Program engagement dashboard: active rate, enrollment, redemption, tier distribution, earn-to-burn, member spend lift (W104.2)
- Program cost reporting: redemption cost, deferred revenue impact, admin cost, promotional cost (W104.3)
- Tier economics analysis: revenue, margin, upgrade/downgrade, retention per tier (W104.4)
- Annual program review with competitive benchmarking and member segmentation (W104.6–7)
- Integration with W17 (day-to-day loyalty operations), W65 (CSAT measurement), W83 (marketing campaigns — loyalty mechanics), W35 (management reporting — program KPIs in executive dashboard)

### Staffing Implication
- **Cost Accountant**: adds ~3 hours/quarter for liability and cost analysis + ~2 hours/year for annual review = ~14 hours/year. Absorbed.
- **Loyalty Manager**: adds ~2 hours/quarter for engagement analysis + ~8 hours/year for annual review = ~16 hours/year. Absorbed.
- **CMO / CFO**: 1 hour/quarter joint review + 2 hours/year annual review = ~6 hours/year. Absorbed.
- **No incremental headcount.**
